History

The Port of Spain Corporation (POSC) is the municipal authority of Port of Spain. It was proclaimed by the then Governor on June 25th, 1914, and formed by the Legislative Council on June 26th, 1914. The corporation has been sustained via numerous legislative acts since then, with the most recent being the Municipal Corporations Act No. 21 of 1990(MCA). The corporation's formal name is "The Mayor, Alderman, Councillors and Citizens of the City of Port of Spain," which was constituted by statute under the Port of Spain Corporation Ordinance (Chapter 39 No. 1, 1950 ED). With this name, the organization shall enjoy eternal succession and the right to sue or be sued in legal actions.

About

The Port of Spain City Corporation (POSCC) is the municipal authority responsible for the capital city of Port of Spain in Trinidad and Tobago. The organization provides local services that are targeted towards improving the quality of life of persons within the city. These services include sanitation, infrastructure, development, and maintenance of public health.
